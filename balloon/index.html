Why launch a balloon?

The Octanis 1 Rover will eventually go to some very cold places. This is for example why we need special batteries that can bear temperatures down to -50°C. But also all the electronic parts need to be working at this kind of conditions, as energy is limited and cannot be wasted on heating the rover. Therefore all components we use are rated to still operate at -40°C or lower.

For testing this temperature limit in a real scenario, we will pack all our electronic boards on a weather balloon and let it rise to about 30 km of altitude. Along with that, we will be able to collect a big amount of sensor data and test the reliability of the LoRa communication link. GPS information, which will be transmitted over a backup GSM connection, will enable us to find the payload again in order to recover the data and hardware.

Launch Date

We will launch the balloon on a day during the Easter Break. The exact date will depend on the weather and the trajectory predictions, so leave us your mail here to stay informed!.

Launch Site

The launch site will be just a 5 minutes walk away from EPFL campus, at the Parc du Russel in Saint-Sulpice. This was necessary because it is the law to be 5 km away from the next airport, which happens to be the "Aéroport de Blecherette" in Lausanne.
